!!! info "What is an open source bounty?"
    An open-source bounty is a monetary reward for completing a task in an
    open-source software project, such as fixing a known bug or implementing a
    new feature. They are one of the many ways of sustaining open-source
    development. By allowing people to get more involved and gain familiarity
    with the code base while remaining focused on real-world challenges,
    projects and their users become more tightly-integrated and efficient.

## The basics

### What is Gitpay?

[Gitpay][] is a funding platform for open-source projects which seeks to match
interested contributors with tasks that users have pledged funds to have solved.
By holding pledged funds in escrow and facilitating communication between all
parties, they work to maximize the probability that submitted work is evaluated
fairly and that contributors are paid promptly for satisfactory solutions.
Account registration is free and permits anyone to list and/or fund a bounty
from any existing GitHub issue.

> RetroArch and the Libretro Team have no formal partnership with Gitpay. Users
> are under no obligation to use the platform to crowdfund work on RetroArch.

## The process

### Stages

1. Users fund bounties on open GitHub issues or feature requests they wish to
   see addressed.
1. Developer(s) create solutions which eliminate the bug or provide the feature
   and file a claim on the corresponding bounty on [Gitpay][gitpay-libretro].
1. Funders review the work and accept or reject the claim. If accepted, Gitpay
   transfers the funds to the developer(s).

## Frequently asked questions

### Who can post a bounty?

Anyone with a credit card issued in a Stripe-supported jurisdiction or a PayPal
account.

### What does it cost to post a bounty?

There is an 8% fee associated with transactions on Gitpay for both funders and
developers.

### Do I need to be affiliated with RetroArch in order fund a bounty?

No. Anyone can put a bounty on any issue, regardless of their relationship to
(or lack thereof) the project.

### Do I need to be affiliated with RetroArch in order to claim a bounty?

No.

### Can several people claim the same bounty?

Yes. When there are multiple claims on a bounty, the people who funded the
bounty decide which of the solutions gets the bounty.

When a bounty claim is submitted by a developer, the claim is put into a
two-week verification period. Backers are notified of the claim via email, and
can then accept or reject it during that period.

- If all backers vote to accept the claim, it is processed immediately and the
  funds are transferred to the contributor.
- If any backer has an issue with the claim, they can choose to reject it.
  Claims cannot be paid out until the dispute is resolved and the rejected
  status is lifted. Gitpay will offer guidance to all parties upon request.
